Open Data China

Advocate Open Data, Cultivate Data Skills, Support Open Innovation

Open Data Defined

"A piece of data or content is open if anyone is free to use, reuse, and redistribute it — subject only, at most, to the requirement to attribute and/or share-alike."

-- OpenDefinition.org

Accessibility and Availability

the data must be publicly available and at no more than a reasonable reproduction cost, preferably via free download over the internet. The data must also be available in a convenient and modifiable form.

Reuse and Redistribution

the data must be provided under terms that permit reuse and redistribution including the intermixing with other datasets. The data must be machine-readable.

Universal Participation

everyone must be able to use, reuse and redistribute – there should be no discrimination against fields of endeavour or against persons or groups.

Our Manifesto

Open Data China is a network of organizations, companies and individuals who share a common brand and stand for common values. This manifesto lays out the 9 points that make us who we are:

  • We are Open Data advocates. We are ordinary citizens, developers, designers, journalists, city planners, data scientists, and people just like you who share a common passion for Open Data.
  • We are not just advocates but serious Doers. We make products, design services, provide trainings, organize meetups, run hackathons, develop tools, and create businesses and organizations that change the world.
  • We believe Open Data means collaboration and decentralization. We have formed this network to facilitate partnerships and collaboration between our independent individual and organizational members.
  • We believe Open Data is a natural resource for innovation.
  • We believe that Open Data gives everyone the equal opportunity and right to use their imagination and skills to innovate and create.
  • We believe data is not just for big players. That’s why we work with citizens, grass-root organizations and start-ups to help them use data to empower themselves.
  • We believe Open Data is a bridge between citizens and government. We want to partner and collaborate with our government to make our country better.
  • We believe that open beats closed. Open source is our default behavior. Our work lives at github.com/opendatachina. Only when we cannot do otherwise will we not publish our work.
  • We believe Open Data means collaboration and decentralization. We have formed this network to facilitate partnerships and collaboration between our independent individual and organizational members.

Initiators
